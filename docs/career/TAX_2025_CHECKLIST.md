## Deduction Maximization Tips for Your Profile

Based on your builder-analyst profile (banking/finance + agentic AI):

### 1. Self-Employment Tax Deduction
- You can deduct 50% of self-employment tax (Schedule SE) — this is automatic but make sure it's claimed

### 2. Home Office Deduction
- **Simplified method**: $5/sq ft, max 300 sq ft = $1,500 deduction
- **Regular method**: Calculate actual expenses (rent/mortgage, utilities, insurance) x business-use %
- Pick whichever is higher

### 3. Section 179 / Bonus Depreciation
- Any computer, monitor, keyboard, etc. purchased in 2025 can be fully deducted in year 1
- Keep receipts from Amazon, Apple, etc.

### 4. Self-Employed Health Insurance
- 100% deductible above-the-line (better than itemizing)
- Includes dental and vision

### 5. Retirement Contributions (HUGE deduction)
- **SEP-IRA**: up to 25% of net self-employment income (max $69,000 for 2025)
- **Solo 401(k)**: up to $23,500 employee + 25% employer
- Can contribute until filing deadline (Apr 15 or Oct 15 with extension)
- **This is often the single biggest deduction for self-employed people**

### 6. Qualified Business Income (QBI) Deduction
- 20% of qualified business income (Section 199A)
- Available if taxable income < $191,950 (single) or < $383,900 (married)
- This is a massive deduction — make sure accountant claims it

### 7. Education & Professional Development
- Courses, books, conferences directly related to your business
- Subscriptions: O'Reilly, ACM, technical newsletters

### 8. Business Use of Vehicle
- If you drive to client meetings, co-working spaces, conferences
- 2025 standard mileage rate: ~70 cents/mile
- Keep a mileage log
