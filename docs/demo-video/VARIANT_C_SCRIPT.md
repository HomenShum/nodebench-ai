## PRODUCTION NOTES

### Why this works (Perplexity/Linear pattern)
- **Contrast is instant comprehension**: The before/after split-screen communicates the value prop in 10 seconds without any explanation. The viewer's brain does the work.
- **Three features, three payoffs**: Each feature hit is self-contained — decision packets, agent orchestration, daily intelligence. Any one of them could be the reason someone signs up.
- **Ad-optimized**: The structure maps directly to paid ad formats. The first 3 seconds hook (chaos), the transition retains, and the features convert. A/B testable — swap feature order, test which converts best.
- **"Free to start" CTA**: Removes friction. The viewer's next thought after seeing this should be "let me try it" not "let me learn more."

### Key difference from original
The original is feature-sequential — it shows features one after another in the order they appear in the app. This variant is **problem-sequential** — it starts with a problem the viewer already has (chaos), shows the solution (contrast), then proves it with 3 rapid hits. The framing is "your life is better with this" not "here's what this does." That's the difference between a product video and a conversion video.

### A/B Testing notes
This variant is designed for paid distribution. Key variables to test:
- **Scene 1 pain point**: Try "47 notifications" vs "Your board meeting is in 3 days" vs "Your co-founder quit"
- **Feature order**: Try leading with agents (Scene 5) instead of decisions (Scene 4)
- **CTA**: Try "Free to start" vs "Ask your first question" vs "See your company in 60 seconds"
- **Length**: Test a 30-second cut (Scenes 1-3 + CTA only) vs the full 58 seconds
